# Low Voltage Directive (LVD) — 2014/35/EU

**Regulation:** Directive 2014/35/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council
**Official text:** [EUR-Lex: 32014L0035](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32014L0035)

---

## Scope

The LVD applies to electrical equipment designed to operate within these voltage ranges:

- **AC:** 50 V to 1 000 V
- **DC:** 75 V to 1 500 V

Typical products in scope include:
- Mains-powered household appliances (washing machines, refrigerators, ovens)
- Power tools and DIY equipment
- Lighting equipment and luminaires
- IT equipment with a mains power supply
- Audio and video equipment with a mains supply
- Industrial and commercial electrical equipment

### What is excluded

The LVD explicitly excludes:
- Equipment for use in explosive atmospheres (covered by ATEX)
- Electro-medical equipment (covered by MDR)
- Electrical parts of lifts (specific lift directive applies)
- Batteries and accumulators as standalone products
- Electricity meters
- Plugs and socket outlets for domestic use

---

## Key obligations for manufacturers

1. **Safety assessment** — Products must be designed and manufactured in accordance with good engineering practice to ensure safety for users and third parties.
2. **Harmonised standards** — Following standards in the LVD Official Journal presumption list (e.g., EN 60335 series for household appliances, EN 62368-1 for audio/video/IT) creates a presumption of conformity.
3. **Technical documentation** — Maintain a technical file including design drawings, risk assessment, test reports, and applied standards.
4. **Declaration of Conformity (DoC)** — Draft and keep an EU Declaration of Conformity. Affix the **CE mark** before placing on the EU market.
5. **Notified Body** — The LVD is a self-declaration directive for most products. A notified body is not normally required unless a harmonised standard is not available.
6. **Labelling** — The CE mark, manufacturer name and address, and product identification must appear on the product or its packaging.

## Voltage reference summary

| Voltage type | Lower limit | Upper limit |
|---|---|---|
| Alternating current (AC) | 50 V | 1 000 V |
| Direct current (DC) | 75 V | 1 500 V |

Mains-powered EU equipment typically operates at 230 V AC (50 Hz), well within LVD scope.

---

## Interaction with other directives

- **EMC Directive (2014/30/EU):** LVD and EMC often apply together. A single CE-marked product typically addresses both.
- **Radio Equipment Directive (2014/53/EU):** Equipment with intentional radio (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) falls under RED, not LVD for radio aspects. However, the mains power supply of a RED product may still need LVD assessment.
- **RoHS Directive (2011/65/EU):** Applies in parallel to most electrical equipment. Same CE mark; separate DoC and substance compliance required.
- **Machinery Regulation:** If the product contains machinery, the Machinery Regulation may also apply.
