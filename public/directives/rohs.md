# RoHS Directive — 2011/65/EU (RoHS 2) as amended by 2015/863/EU (RoHS 3)

**Regulation:** Directive 2011/65/EU, amended by Directive (EU) 2015/863
**Official text:** [EUR-Lex: 32011L0065](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32011L0065)

---

## Scope

The RoHS Directive restricts the use of certain **hazardous substances** in electrical and electronic equipment (EEE) placed on the EU market.

**Categories of equipment in scope (Annex I):**
1. Large household appliances
2. Small household appliances
3. IT and telecommunications equipment
4. Consumer equipment
5. Lighting equipment
6. Electrical and electronic tools
7. Toys, leisure, and sports equipment
8. Medical devices
9. Monitoring and control instruments (including industrial)
10. Automatic dispensers
11. Other EEE not covered by the above categories

In practice, RoHS applies to virtually all electrical and electronic equipment sold to end users in the EU, including B2B products.

---

## Restricted substances (Annex II)

| Substance | Maximum concentration (by weight in homogeneous material) |
|---|---|
| Lead (Pb) | 0.1 % |
| Mercury (Hg) | 0.1 % |
| Cadmium (Cd) | 0.01 % |
| Hexavalent chromium (Cr VI) | 0.1 % |
| Polybrominated biphenyls (PBB) | 0.1 % |
| Polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDE) | 0.1 % |
| Bis(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP) | 0.1 % |
| Butyl benzyl phthalate (BBP) | 0.1 % |
| Dibutyl phthalate (DBP) | 0.1 % |
| Diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP) | 0.1 % |

The last four phthalates were added by the RoHS 3 amendment (2015/863/EU), applicable from **22 July 2019**.

---

## Key obligations for manufacturers

1. **Substance compliance** — Ensure no restricted substance exceeds its limit in any homogeneous material of the product.
2. **Supply chain due diligence** — Obtain substance declarations from component and material suppliers (IEC 62474 material declarations are common).
3. **Technical documentation** — Maintain records of substance assessments, test results (XRF screening or chemical analysis), and supplier declarations.
4. **EU Declaration of Conformity** — The RoHS DoC can be combined with other directives (e.g., LVD, EMC). It must reference the RoHS Directive and confirm compliance.
5. **CE marking** — The CE mark covers RoHS compliance. No notified body is required.
6. **Exemptions** — Annex III (general exemptions) and Annex IV (medical/monitoring exemptions) list permitted uses above the thresholds. Exemptions are time-limited and subject to renewal.

---

## Exemptions overview

Common Annex III exemptions include:
- Lead in solders for servers, storage, telecom infrastructure (expiry dates vary)
- Mercury in fluorescent lamps and discharge lamps (expiry dates vary)
- Lead in high-temperature solders (above 85°C melting point)

Check the current exemption status on the [EUR-Lex RoHS exemption page](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32011L0065) before relying on any exemption — many have lapsed or been amended.

## Interaction with other regulations

- **REACH (EC 1907/2006):** REACH Annex XVII contains use restrictions for some of the same substances. REACH SVHC notifications (Annex XIV) may also apply if substances of very high concern are present above 0.1 % in the product. RoHS and REACH obligations run in parallel.
- **WEEE Directive (2012/19/EU):** RoHS and WEEE are companion directives — both apply to EEE. RoHS addresses substance restrictions; WEEE addresses end-of-life recovery and crossed-bin labelling.
- **Battery Regulation (EU 2023/1542):** Batteries in products are subject to the Battery Regulation substance restrictions in addition to RoHS. The Battery Regulation introduces specific substance limits and labelling for batteries.
