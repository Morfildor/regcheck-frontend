# Radio Equipment Directive (RED) — 2014/53/EU

**Regulation:** Directive 2014/53/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council
**Official text:** [EUR-Lex: 32014L0053](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32014L0053)

---

## Scope

The RED applies to any product that intentionally emits or receives **radio waves** for the purpose of radio communication or radio determination. This covers a very wide range of products:

- Wi-Fi devices (routers, smart home controllers, appliances)
- Bluetooth and Bluetooth LE accessories
- Cellular (3G/4G/5G/LTE) connected devices
- NFC-enabled products
- Zigbee, Z-Wave, and similar short-range devices
- GPS and GNSS receivers
- Amateur radio equipment
- Emergency radio equipment

### What is excluded

- Devices that use radio frequencies below 9 kHz (e.g., some industrial induction equipment)
- Marine equipment under the MED Directive
- Aviation equipment
- Custom-built R&D equipment used exclusively by research laboratories

---

## Essential requirements (Article 3)

All RED products must meet:

**Article 3(1)(a) — Health and safety:**
Products must protect the health and safety of persons. This generally references the LVD safety requirements for powered radio equipment.

**Article 3(1)(b) — EMC:**
Products must not cause harmful interference and must be constructed to withstand reasonable levels of electromagnetic disturbance.

**Article 3(2) — Efficient use of radio spectrum:**
Products must make efficient use of radio spectrum to avoid harmful interference.

### Cybersecurity delegated act — Article 3(3)(d/e/f)

The RED Cybersecurity Delegated Regulation (EU) 2022/30 (in force from **1 August 2025**) adds mandatory requirements for:

- **Internet-connected radio equipment** (Article 3(3)(d)): Must not harm the network or its functioning.
- **Equipment processing personal data** (Article 3(3)(e)): Must protect privacy; incorporates data minimisation, data protection by design.
- **Child-directed or toy equipment** (Article 3(3)(f)): Specific security requirements for equipment for children.

Products with Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or NFC that connect to the internet or process personal data are typically in scope for the delegated act.

---

## Key obligations for manufacturers

1. **Conformity assessment** — For most RED products, the manufacturer can self-declare conformity using harmonised standards. A notified body is required if no harmonised standard covers the radio type.
2. **Technical documentation** — Must include radio test reports, a description of the radio features, and the frequency bands used.
3. **EU Declaration of Conformity** — Must list the RED and all essential requirements addressed, including the Article 3(3) cybersecurity requirements if applicable.
4. **CE marking and product marking** — The CE mark is required. Products with restricted frequency bands in some EU Member States must display the alert sign (exclamation mark in circle) on the packaging.
5. **ETSI registration** — For some product types, ETSI notification or type approval may be required. Check the relevant harmonised standard.
6. **Software-defined radio (SDR)** — If the radio parameters can be changed by software, this must be documented; Class 2 notification may be required.

## Interaction with other directives

- **LVD:** Mains-powered RED products (e.g., a Wi-Fi router with an integrated power supply) need LVD compliance for their electrical safety aspects. The RED essential requirement 3(1)(a) references LVD-level safety but does not replace LVD for products above the LVD voltage thresholds.
- **EMC Directive:** The RED replaces the EMC Directive for radio equipment. The EMC Directive does not apply to the radio parts of RED products, though it may still apply to non-radio sub-assemblies if sold separately.
- **CRA (EU 2024/2847):** The Cyber Resilience Act will supplement the RED cybersecurity delegated act for products with digital elements. The CRA obligations apply from **December 2027** for most product classes.
