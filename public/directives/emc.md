# Electromagnetic Compatibility Directive (EMC) — 2014/30/EU

**Regulation:** Directive 2014/30/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council
**Official text:** [EUR-Lex: 32014L0030](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32014L0030)

---

## Scope

The EMC Directive applies to all **electrical and electronic apparatus** that can cause electromagnetic disturbance or whose performance can be affected by it.

Products in scope include:
- Consumer electronics (TVs, computers, home audio)
- Industrial machinery and control equipment
- Power conversion equipment
- Lighting equipment with electronic control gear
- ICT equipment
- Automotive components (some overlap with ECE regulations)

### The two EMC obligations

**Emissions** — The apparatus must not generate electromagnetic disturbance above prescribed limits that could affect other equipment.

**Immunity** — The apparatus must withstand electromagnetic disturbance in its intended environment without unacceptable degradation of performance.

### What is excluded

- Radio and telecommunications equipment falling entirely under RED (2014/53/EU)
- Aeronautical and marine products under specific sector legislation
- Custom-built equipment for professional use in professional research/development facilities

---

## Key obligations for manufacturers

1. **EMC assessment** — Conduct a conformity assessment by either following harmonised standards or applying a technical construction file approach with a notified body.
2. **Harmonised standards** — The EN 55032 (emissions) and EN 55035 (immunity) series cover most generic categories. Product-family standards (e.g., EN 60335 for appliances) may supersede the generic standards.
3. **Technical documentation** — Keep a technical file including EMC design measures, test reports, and a description of precautions taken.
4. **EU Declaration of Conformity** — Required before CE marking. The DoC must list the EMC Directive and any harmonised standards applied.
5. **CE marking** — Affix CE mark; no notified body is normally required if using harmonised standards.
6. **Fixed installation exemption** — Apparatus intended only for installation into a fixed installation is exempt from CE marking but must comply with EMC requirements and carry documentation at the installation.

## Interaction with other directives

- **LVD (2014/35/EU):** Mains-powered products often require both LVD and EMC compliance. Both are covered by a single CE mark but separate DoC sections or a combined DoC.
- **RED (2014/53/EU):** Equipment with intentional radio falls under RED. The RED covers essential requirements that include EMC aspects for radio products, so the EMC Directive does not apply separately to the radio parts. The non-radio parts of a device (e.g., mains power supply) may still need LVD but not EMC separately.
- **Machinery Regulation:** EMC requirements are referenced in the Machinery Regulation; the EMC Directive may not apply to machinery where Machinery covers EMC aspects.
